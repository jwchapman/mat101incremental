About This Game
Welcome
Welcome to College Algebra Incremental! This game was modeled off the incremental/"idle" game genre. The gameplay of incremental games tends to boil down to performing various simple actions to make some number get larger and larger. On the face of it, this sounds very boring. But deep down, we all have a subconscious desire to see ourselves make progress and achieve things. Who doesn't like to finish just one more semester, one more presentation, one more day at work, one more homework assignment, one more [whatever it might be]? Whenever we accomplish something, it gives us the momentum we need to go accomplish more things.

Why Play This Game?
If you just learned about this game, then you are likely taking College Algebra this semester. In case you don't know, we learn math by doing math, but a lot of people lack the motivation to do math. Enter the incremental game. Need motivation to do something? Why not attach the psychological element of seeing a number go up, seeing progress made, and getting the resulting momentum to help keep us going further? Who knows, by the end of the semester, you might actually be enjoying math.

What Browsers/Platforms Can I Play On, and Can I Transfer Progress?
This game will run fine in most modern browsers: Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari. I'm not an expert on the nuances of different browsers, so if one doesn't work, try Chrome for sure.
Create a backup often in case you run into a problem with your particular browser. Restoring a backup file is also how you can transfer your progress to another browser.
While this game might run on a phone or tablet device, I doubt the experience would be as nice as it would be on the large screen of a laptop or desktop computer.

Getting Started
In this game, you will be accumulating "Knowledge". This is the number that will be going up. You must start the game by doing 10 basic algebra review questions. Once you have accumulated 10 Knowledge, you will be able to unlock Unit 1 and begin the main part of the game.

Once you have unlocked Unit 1, you can purchase any type of question you can afford (with Knowledge). If you answer the question correctly, you will be awarded with a certain amount of Knowledge per second (KPS). From then on, Knowledge will accrue passively. So why will you ever need to play the game any more? Because the successive types of questions cost exponentially more Knowledge, and you will only ever accrue Knowledge linearly if you are idle (not actively playing and answering questions). For example, it might take you 1 hour to afford a certain question type if you just let the game run. But it will take you 7 hours to afford the one after that, and over 2 days to afford the one after that. So to make any progress in this game, you will have to check in every now and then and answer questions!

To unlock Unit 2 (through Unit 13), you will need to meet certain milestones: (1) a Knowledge requirement, (2) a KPS requirement, and (3) a number-of-questions-correct requirement.

In addition to the next problem type being exponentially more expensive, every time you purchase a question, the next question of that same type will be 20% more costly. If you get a question wrong, of course you won't get the KPS reward, but there is no penalty other than the usual fact that the next question is 20% more expensive.

Upgrade System
What do the background colors in the "Purchase" column mean when you answer questions correctly? Answer your first question of a certain type correctly, and you attain "Copper" level. Is this a glorious achievement? Well let's remind ourselves that pennies are made of copper.

Once you answer ten of a type correctly, you will trigger an upgrade that you can purchase in the Upgrade box. If you purchase this upgrade, you will attain "Silver" level for that question type, along with a "x2" KPS boost for that type.

There are a few more levels to progress through for each question type. Buying these upgrades is optional, but you may find that doing some of them helps you progress more quickly. Here are the levels to unlock:

If you can make it to diamond level for a question type, you get the x1000 KPS multiplier for that question type, but your global KPS (for all question types) will be instantly boosted by 20%.

There are also some special upgrades you can buy, but I won't spoil the surprise. They will make themselves known to you in due time.

Greek Letter Clickables
Every so often, you might see a golden, lowercase, Greek letter pop up and slowly dance on your screen. Click on it, of course! It will reward you with something nice. It won't hang around forever, so during your active play time, be on the lookout. Can you find all 24 Greek letters? Check out the Statistics tab to see which ones you have (green) and which ones you are missing (red).

Rubies
If you leave your game running idle for hours, you will come back to a lot of Knowledge. (If only acquiring real knowledge worked that way.) Yes, you could plow on ahead into the next unit, but you probably haven't learned that stuff yet.

So what to do with all your newfound Knowledge? Buy rubies!! Each ruby costs 2 hours' worth of your KPS, so they really are meant to be a resource that you buy after being idle for some time, not something that you spend your active time saving up for. So go to bed, wake up, and buy rubies! There is also a small chance to get a ruby from a Greek letter! Oh, did I mention that each ruby adds 1% to your base KPS? So yeah, they have a benefit. Can you be the first to collect 500 rubies?

Achievements
There are 450 achievements in this game, so check out the tab. You will get a pop-up each time you get a new achievement. You can dismiss it by clicking. Can you unlock all 450? This is no small feat. You'll have to answer A LOT of questions to get there, but by then you will be a college algebra expert!

So I Should Just Leave The Game Running?
Absolutely! This is how you play idle games. I use a laptop that I rarely turn off but rather just close the lid so it sleeps. Just keep the game open in a tab. Don't close the tab, but let the computer sleep. When you come back, the game should have made progress. The only time the game won't make progress is if you close the tab or the browser altogether (including turning off your computer).

Saving Your Progress
You will undoubtedly invest a good amount of time into this game, both actively and passively. It would be a shame if something happens and you lose all your progress. Luckily, this game autosaves your progress every second in your browser's localStorage.

HOWEVER, things can still go wrong, and it could either be your fault or my fault. Even if I were a professional programmer, I could have overlooked some bugs in this game. Something might break your game and render it unplayable. You might also clear some of your browser settings and unknowingly delete your game progress. So I have put in a backup/restore feature as well. I recommend AT LEAST ONCE A DAY (or however often you feel you have made significant progress that you wouldn't want to lose), click the "Create Backup" button in the menu at the top of the page. A single click of this button will download a .txt file that you can use to restore your progress through the "Restore" button, should the unthinkable ever happen.

Also, your game should continue to play even if you switch browser tabs. If you close the game's tab by accident, don't fret. Remember, it autosaved less than a second ago.

Hard Reset
Tired of having made so much progress? Wipe your game and start completely over using the Hard Reset button. Don't worry, if you click it by accident, it's ok. It asks to make sure you're serious about such a big decision first.

What If You Find a Bug
If you find a bug or exploit in the game, kindly let me know by sending an email to jacobDOTchapmanATusmDOTedu. (You should be able to decipher that email address.) Since the game progress is saved on your machine and not on a server, there is nothing I can do if you lose your progress somehow, so backup often!

If you have any comments or suggestions, feel free to pass them along as well. I'd love to hear how you are enjoying the game (or not enjoying the game).
